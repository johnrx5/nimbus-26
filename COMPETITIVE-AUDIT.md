## VISUAL DESIGN PATTERNS (What MyDose Does Well)

1. **Expandable cards everywhere** — pack info density without overwhelming on first view
2. **Real product screenshots** — they show the actual dashboard UI, not abstract illustrations
3. **Numbered step flows** — journey visualization is their core storytelling device
4. **Light/airy design** — white backgrounds, blue/purple gradients, soft blob shapes
5. **Audience-specific pages** — separate pages for clinics, patients, partners
6. **"X, not Y" framing** — positions against status quo without naming competitors
7. **Anti-personas** — "Not ideal for..." builds credibility
8. **Extremely detailed copy** — they're not afraid of long-form text within expandable sections
9. **Chat UI mockups** — AI features shown as simulated conversations
10. **Pricing transparency** — per-patient pricing with interactive calculator

## WHAT THEY'RE MISSING (Our Advantage)

1. **No owned pharmacy** — they "integrate with" 503A/503B pharmacies. We ARE the pharmacy.
2. **No visible leadership** — no named medical board, no credentials, no "who's behind this"
3. **No telehealth page** (under construction) — we have telehealth live
4. **No pharmacies page** (under construction) — we own Lake Hills Pharmacy
5. **No about page** (under construction) — no company story
6. **No case studies** (under construction) — no social proof
7. **Light on real testimonials** — we have named doctors
8. **No real metrics** — no "X clinics", "Y patients", "Z states"
9. **Generic illustrations** — while they show screenshots, they don't show REAL clinic environments
10. **No compliance depth** — they mention it but don't lead with it like we should
