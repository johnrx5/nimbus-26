**Introduction**

Your grandfather likely had higher testosterone levels than you do today — even with cigars, hard work, and no fancy supplements. Research confirms that men's testosterone has dropped around **20-30% since the 1970s**, aside from what natural aging would predict.

This isn't just about getting older faster — it's a men's health concern that affects energy, metabolic health, mood, and longevity.

**The Science Behind the Decline**

While testosterone naturally declines by approximately **1% per year after age 30**, modern men are experiencing even steeper declines.

- Cleveland Clinic notes the typical drop of 1% per year after age 30.
- A Boston-area cohort study found substantial declines **not explained by obesity, smoking, or other health variables**.
- Broader research confirms similar secular trends globally.

**Why It Matters for Modern Men**

Low testosterone deeply affects:

- **Physical health**: loss of muscle mass, increased fat accumulation, reduced bone density.
- **Mental well-being**: testosterone plays a role in memory, mood, and brain health.
- **Cardiometabolic risks**: low testosterone raises the likelihood of metabolic syndrome, depression, and cardiovascular disease.

**Why the Decline Has Accelerated**

- **Obesity and sedentary lifestyles**
- **Environmental toxins**, including BPA and phthalates
- **Sleep deprivation, chronic stress, and poor nutrition**
