## Consumption of Ultra-processed Food and Risk of Depression

Previous investigations established connections between elevated ultra-processed food (UPF) consumption and metabolic conditions including obesity, hypertension, diabetes, and dyslipidemia. This research examined whether UPF intake relates to newly occurring depression.

When compared against those with minimal UPF intake, the highest-consumption group exhibited a 49% increased risk of depression. Reducing UPF consumption by three daily servings correlated with a 16% lower risk of depression.

Additional investigation remains necessary, though current evidence indicates that deriving most nutritional calories from unprocessed and whole foods supports comprehensive wellness.

## Presence and Quantity of Botanical Ingredients in Sports Supplements

The supplement sector operates under FDA standards, though regulatory oversight occurs only following formal complaints. The FDA neither pre-approves active ingredients nor mandates safety or efficacy documentation before market introduction.

Recent analyses revealed that merely 11% contained the component in an amount within 10% of what was on the label, while 12% had products that the FDA banned. Additionally, 40% did not contain any detectable amount of the active ingredient.
